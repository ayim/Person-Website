Founding the Military Band

Awarded the Lieutenant-General J.W. Quinn Annual Awards for Excellence in Music.

Context

At my Cadet Corps, I had participated in an earlier attempt to form a massed band with another unit. After that effort had collapsed, I worked towards my drum major qualification while volunteering to help start a found a band that belonged to the cadet unit.

Problem

As we lacked percussionists, our monthly performances were tough. Morale was low, and it was clear to both the band, and the cadet unit that our new team needed traction.

Solution

With the help of other senior cadets, we created branding assets to help us in our active promotion of the benefits of joining our new band. Combined with speakers from other successful military bands in the local area, member enrollment increased significantly.

The strongest musicians within the new membership were given further training, and roles as section leaders to provide other members mentorship. With their hard work and the grit of our members, our tunes became more musical. With everyone's help, we dared to take crazy ideas and put them together in a marching sequence for our very first Band Competition. There, as the only Army Cadets on parade, we brought home a Silver standing.

Impact

Today, our success is reflected in the interest shown towards the music program. In addition to their regular training on Tuesday nights, the cadets dedicate another 3 hours per week to band practices. As pioneers within the cadet unit, the 3 extremely qualified replacements who I mentor twice a week and numerous cadets that regularly attend summer music camp are proof of the band's success in growth. pioneers within our corps, but we shared our passion for music within our province.

Steveston London Student Council

Media Team

Envisioned, trained, and directed a media team to coordinate event logistics, audio visual support, and performer training.

Problem

My high school, Steveston-London Secondary has traditionally had a very strong performing culture. The year before I became an executive, the sole member with media experience had graduated, and as such, large events that required A/V support simply weren't possible.

Solution

By combining audio-visual training taken during elementary school and project management experience from Cadets, I founded an event support crew that coordinates logistics, audio visual support, and handles communication with performers in order to help events run smoothly. The team serves 30+ events per year.

Impact

Although the role of tech crew is gritty at times, I enjoy watching performances unfold with my help. By establishing a training and execution framework for coordinating audio visual support, what was originally a solo operation has since expanded to a group of twelve.

Not only did we turn a 90 minute overrun time of our largest annual event into a surplus of 15 minutes, future members of have gone on to acquire DJ and lighting equipment to further improve production quality.
