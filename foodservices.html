Keeping UWaterloo well fed

Problem Space

Between menu hell and copius amounts of scrolling, the fundamental usability of UWaterloo food services' website is challenged. Key information about opening hours and dietary values are not provided in a way that meets busy students' needs.

With food being a physical requirement for human survival, students prefer to visit off campus food outlets. Thus, our high level goals were to help UW Food Services achieve the following:

  1. Give users the certainty of arriving at an open on-campus food outlet
  2. Make it fast and easy to find a meal that suits users' dietary preferences
  3. Gather user feedback to help improve product offerings

My Role

As a part of a four person group, my role was not only to to create a survey and analyze its responses to inform design goals, but also to create the UI/UX design.

Validating the problem

With the help of an online survey, we gathered information about 186 students. Early results confirmed our assumptions towards the problem.

Technological Insight
70% of users browse the Food Services website on mobile.
The top three tasks carried out by users on the website were as follows:
  1. Check opening hours.
  2. View the weekly menu.
  3. Check nutritional information.
User Insight
76% have been to a location they didn't expect to be closed.
Although the top user goal was to check opening hours, the website has clearly failed in its mission.
Deeper Insights

Digging into the data revealed a mismatch between what customers wanted, and what UW Food Services was offering.

Market Insight

Although location is the 2nd most important factor, food quality is king.
Two-thirds of survey respondents actually prefer dining off campus or at home despite the inconvenience. Survey results towards restaurant selection criteria identifies food quality as the #1 most important factor contributing to this. It is the only criteria where no respondents indicated that it did not matter, or doesn't matter much.
Takeaway
UW Food Services needs to gather user feedback in order to improve food quality and customer satisfaction.
User Insight

The majority of users are happy to leave reviews.. but only if prompted.
Only 27% indicated they would leave a review without being prompted, but this number rose to 62% if users were to be prompted.
More interestingly, only 18% of users indicated that reviews would be extremely helpful in selecting a dish. This suggests that the large majority of customers prefer to buy from select types of dishes that they prefer to consume regardless of what others think.
Takeaway
UW Food Services may need to further investigate how they can better improve their product mix.
User Insight

Students are creatures of habit.
Surprisingly, 80% of students indicate that they usually know which location they intend to dine at.
To add to this, more than 50% usually know which dish they intend to order.
Takeaway
Location discovery is not as important as helping users find a food item that suits thems.
Introducing UW Food Finder.

Enabling creatures of habit.
UW Food Finder immediately helps determine whether your top three locations are open.
One tap access to daily menus helps you find the dish you're craving.
No more scrolling through long lists to find the same information you're looking for everytime.
making it easy to fulfill your needs.
Let others help you pick from today's top rated dishes or quickly filter to find one that suits you.
